VOLUNTEER GUIDE

Join the Code Crunch volunteer collective and help shape the future of CS students during the School year and hackathons. Complete the volunteer process to get confirmed and start your mission.

VOLUNTEER PROCESS

[ Required for ALL volunteers ] - Essential steps for every volunteer mission

  1. 1
    Read the Wiki
    Review the Volunteer Wiki for complete information on onboarding, forms, certificates, and volunteering guidelines.
    Access Volunteer Wiki
    Essential reading - contains all detailed information about volunteer processes, requirements, and guidelines.
  2. 2
    Apply
    Complete the Volunteer Application (Form No. 1) to sign up as a judge, mentor, speaker, or other volunteer.
    Access Volunteer Application (Form No. 1)
    Only one submission needed per role. If your info changes, submit a new form — the most recent version will be used.
  3. 3
    Get Confirmed
    After your application is reviewed, you'll receive the Volunteer Confirmation Form (Form No. 2) by email. Use this to confirm your participation, assigned role, and event details.
    Resubmit if your availability changes at least 10 days before the event.
  4. 4
    Speakers & Mentors (Required)
    If you are a speaker or mentor:
    • Use Form No. 3 to propose your Speaker or Workshop session (technical or non-technical)
    Access Speaker/Workshop Form (Form No. 3)
    • Use Form No. 4 to share your Mentor Time-Slot Availability so we can match you with students
    Access Mentor Availability Form (Form No. 4)
  5. 5
    For Industry Leaders (Optional)
    Industry professionals may also:
    • Use Form No. 5 to join as an Industry Leader Organizer (help plan events, logistics, partnerships)
    Access Industry Leader Organizer Form (Form No. 5)
    • Use Form No. 6 to join our Fundraising Advisory Board (support sponsorship outreach and fundraising)
    Access Fundraising Advisory Board Form (Form No. 6)
  6. 6
    Join the Community
    • Join Discord using the invite link emailed to you after confirmation
    • Share your volunteer role on LinkedIn and connect with the Code Crunch network
    Follow Code Crunch on LinkedIn
Complete all required steps to be fully confirmed and ready to volunteer.

INDUSTRY PROFESSIONAL FAQ

[ EXECUTIVE BRIEFING ] - Strategic information for industry professionals and tech experts

After being accepted, your role participation for judging, mentoring, or speaking will be confirmed through your response on Form No. 2 (sent after acceptance as volunteer). It is important you join Discord as communication through email will be minimal due to high volume of volunteers.

Important Notes:
• Form No. 1 is just an interest form to know headcount and evaluate volunteers - IT DOES NOT confirm participation, only Form No. 2 does
• If something changes and you cannot participate anymore, please message on DM or tag hack-organizers on private volunteer channel
• We might not have daily updated participation on DevPost or hack page for in-person volunteers, but you can always use your latest response on Form No. 2 as reference

Timeline:
• 1 day before hack: We finalize participation with all mentors and speakers who confirmed hours (Form No. 4 for mentors, Form No. 3 for speakers)
• Non-responsive volunteers will be removed since we need this information for participants
• For judges: You'll get access when the hack opens to the EXPO, or prior on rolling basis - use Form No. 2 if you haven't received access since sometimes it's due to missing your confirmation

Check Form No. 2 to make sure you confirmed you will be a judge or that you completed the form correctly. If you are in the form and your selection is "Yes for judge" in the desired hackathon, reach out to us. Final access will be granted when the hackathon starts, so if your entries are correct in the form, please reach out. All communication after volunteer role confirmation is managed primarily on Discord - make sure you have done your intro on the server to be added to the private channel. Secondary channel is LinkedIn, and last channel is email (but not frequently monitored), so make sure to join Discord to stay in the loop.

Check your form submissions here:

• Interest Form (Form No. 1): codecrunchvolunteers-formno1-respon.vercel.app

• Volunteer Confirmation (Form No. 2): codecrunchvolunteers-formno2-respon.vercel.app

• Technical Workshop (Form No. 3): codecrunchvolunteers-formno3-respon.vercel.app

• Mentor Availability (Form No. 4): codecrunchvolunteers-formno4-respon.vercel.app

• Organizer Form (Form No. 5): codecrunchvolunteers-formno5-respon.vercel.app

• Advisory Board/Fundraiser (Form No. 6): codecrunchvolunteers-formno6-respon.vercel.app

Note: Some responses might not appear in the forms, but you will always receive a copy of your responses after each form submission - check your spam folder. For any questions, reach us on LinkedIn.

Each judge will be assigned a minimum of 10 projects from random tracks to ensure fair evaluation across all submissions. If you wish to evaluate more projects or specific track projects, this is possible but must be informed with adequate time in advance. Your request will be approved on top of the 10 minimum projects, allowing you to contribute additional expertise where most valuable.

The challenge prompt will be unveiled on the day the hackathon starts. Each hackathon has track descriptions available beforehand, but the specific prompt is revealed at the event launch. Projects can vary by major since students choose their final focus for a specific area based on their interests and expertise.

If you're not listed on DevPost with your volunteer role: This means you didn't confirm with Form No. 2 and didn't join Discord.

If you haven't received expo updates: Most likely you didn't complete Form No. 2.

Check your responses:
• Form No. 2 responses: codecrunchvolunteers-formno2-respon.vercel.app
• Form No. 3 responses: codecrunchvolunteers-formno3-respon.vercel.app
• Form No. 4 responses: codecrunchvolunteers-formno4-respon.vercel.app

Missing speaker/mentor hours? You didn't fill Form No. 3 and Form No. 4, and didn't respond to Code Crunch team follow-ups to finalize your participation.

All judge evaluation criteria and guidelines can be found in our Hack Toolkit accessible through the menu or directly at hackuniversityhacktoolkit.vercel.app. This comprehensive resource includes scoring rubrics, evaluation frameworks, and best practices for fair and effective project assessment.

Industry professionals can serve as judges, mentors, and technical workshop hosts to directly connect with students, help them grow, and support emerging talent. You also have exclusive access to advanced roles including Industry Leader Organizer (Form No. 5) where you help plan events, manage logistics, and develop partnerships. You can also join our Fundraising Advisory Board (Form No. 6) to support sponsorship outreach and strategic fundraising initiatives. These roles are perfect for those with a passion for leadership and student development.

Volunteering with Code Crunch provides thought leadership opportunities, meaningful connections with passionate students, and the satisfaction of supporting new talent. You'll help students grow through direct mentorship, gain insights into student innovations and trends, and contribute to building the next generation of tech professionals. In-person volunteers are featured on event websites, and all volunteers are displayed on DevPost event pages for recognition.

Industry professional roles are designed to be executive-friendly. Most commitments range from 2-5 hours per month for advisory roles, or 1-2 days for major hackathon events. Organizer roles may require 3-8 hours monthly depending on your involvement level. We work around your schedule and respect C-suite time constraints.

Absolutely! Industry professionals often serve as sponsorship ambassadors, connecting Code Crunch with their professional networks. As a Fundraising Advisory Board member, you'll help identify partnership opportunities, facilitate corporate sponsorships, and leverage your industry connections to expand our resource base and student opportunities.

We particularly value professionals with expertise in AI/ML, cybersecurity, fintech, cloud computing, data science, product management, startup scaling, and emerging technologies. As an interdisciplinary club, we also welcome non-tech majors including business, design, marketing, psychology, and liberal arts professionals. We need strategic guidance in areas like corporate partnerships, event management, marketing, legal compliance, educational program development, and user experience design.

Industry volunteers get early access to top student talent through mentoring relationships and project evaluations. Many companies recruit directly from our hackathons and workshops. You'll interact with students from FIU and universities worldwide, identifying potential interns, new graduates, and innovative thinkers before they hit the general job market.

Industry professionals receive executive-level recognition including featured profiles on our website (Only in-person volunteer), LinkedIn post mentions (If apliicable), speaker visibility for events, and Certificate for susecfull participation. Advisory board members receive official titles and can leverage this experience for board positions and industry leadership roles.

Most industry professional roles are fully remote-friendly. Advisory board meetings are conducted virtually, and you can mentor/judge remotely. Organizer roles may benefit from occasional in-person participation for major Miami events, but virtual contribution is valued and accommodated for global industry professionals.

The link is sent after successful completion of Form No. 2.

Industry Leader Organizers (Form No. 5): Hands-on event planning, logistics coordination, partnership development, and program strategy. More tactical involvement in day-to-day operations.

Fundraising Advisory Board (Form No. 6): Strategic guidance on sponsorship strategy, network introductions, fundraising campaigns, and high-level partnership negotiations. Advisory board members also help with workshop creation and guidance, support student-led workshops, or lead them with the opportunity to provide students certificates upon completion. More strategic, advisory-focused role.
