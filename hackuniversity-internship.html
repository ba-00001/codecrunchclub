Program Overview

The Code Crunch Internship Program is a 10-week remote or hybrid initiative designed to engage students from diverse majors — including Business, Nursing, Psychology, Criminal Justice, Finance, Behavioral Science, Data Science, Cybersecurity, Artificial Intelligence, Cloud Computing, Software Engineering, Civil Engineering, Biomedical Engineering, Computer Engineering, Electrical Engineering, and more — in solving real-world challenges using GenAI and research-based strategies. It is offered in both English and Spanish and open to students across the U.S., Latin America, and beyond.

For Students

  • You will be matched with a real company problem or challenge.
  • Hack (problem-solve a solution) and design a 10-week roadmap to solve it using GenAI, research, code, or major-specific tools.
  • Submit weekly progress every week in two sync meetings, broadcasted and recorded on our YouTube channel through 30-minute presentations.
  • Required time commitment: minimum 10 hrs, maximum 20 hrs weekly.
  • Every hour you work in the internship program must be logged. Log your work every 10 hours.
  • Be available to meet weekly with a mentor for 1 hour and get feedback and guidance.
  • Final showcase presentation at the end of the 10 weeks.
  • Note: Program start dates are flexible depending on student, organization, and mentor availability.

For Companies

  • Submit real-world problems for students to tackle.
  • Receive fresh solutions and proposals from cross-disciplinary interns.
  • Support the program with donations to sustain Code Crunch initiatives.
  • Recognize and highlight student contributors.

For Industry Mentors

  • Volunteer 1 hour/week to mentor a student.
  • Review logs and presentations.
  • Offer insights, feedback, and career advice.
  • Make a lasting impact in 10 weeks.
